Assessment Cues

S: The mother verbalized, “Naubo padin siya tas may kasamang plema.”

O: T: 36.8°C, PR: 121 bpm, RR: 28 cpm, SpO2: 98%

(+) productive cough, (+) crackles on both sides

Nursing Diagnosis

Ineffective Airway Clearance r/t retained secretions, as evidenced by adventitious lung sounds (crackles).

Objectives of Care

Short Term: By the end of the 8-hour shift, the patient will have improved airway clearance as evidenced by reduced congestion, clearer breath sounds, and diminished crackles.

Long Term: Within the next 2-3 weeks the patient will maintain a clear airway as evidenced by normal breath sounds, effective coughing, absence of congestion, and adequate oxygenation without respiratory distress.

Plan of Interventions

Dx:

- The nurse will monitor VS and INO every 4 hours.

- Monitor 02 SAT every 4 hours.

- Auscultate the lungs for abnormal sounds: crackles, wheezing, rhonchi.

- Monitor for signs of difficulty in breathing: cyanotic, use of accessory muscles, nasal flaring, chest retractions.

Tx:

- Perform CPT as needed.

- Administer medication for relief of respiratory tract infection: Disudrin 1mL drops and Salbutamol 1 nebule as prescribed by the physician.

- Administer antibiotics Ceftriaxone 869 mg IV as prescribed by the physician.

Ed:

- Encourage increased fluid intake.

- Educate the patient's parents on the proper way of functioning: Gently compress and release the bulb.

- Advise to watch out for signs of difficulty in breathing: paleness, use of accessory muscles, nasal flaring, chest retractions.

- Advice to gently do percussion/ tap the patients back as needed.

Rationale

Dx:

Monitor V/S and I & O

- To assess and make sure the pt's VS stays within normal ranges and to track fluid balance and prevent and detect early signs of dehydration.

Monitor O2 Sat

- Check for signs of difficulty in breathing.

Auscultate the lungs

- To help detect fluid, airway narrowing, or mucus buildup, indicating respiratory issues.

Monitor for signs of difficulty in breathing:

- To help identify respiratory distress early, allowing timely intervention to prevent worsening of conditions.

Tx:

Perform CPT as needed.

- Aid in clearing mucus from the lungs, improving airflow and oxygenation.

Administer medication

- To reduce nasal congestion (Disudrin) and improve breathing by relaxing airways (Salbutamol).

Administer antibiotics

- To help fight bacterial infections.

Ed:

Encourage increased fluid intake.

- To avoid signs of dehydration.

Educate the patient's parents

- Help clear secretions, ensuring effective airway clearance and prevent difficulty in breathing.

Advise to watch out for signs of difficulty

- Allows early intervention, preventing complications and ensuring timely medical care.

Advice to gently do percussion/ tap the patients back as needed.

- To loosen the mucus in the lungs.

Assessment Cues

S: The mother verbalized, “Malambot tae niya.”

O: T: 36.8°C, PR: 121 bpm, RR: 28 cpm

Nursing Diagnosis

Risk for electrolyte imbalance r/t excessive fluid loss secondary to diarrhea.

Objectives of Care

Short Term: Within the 8-hour shift, the patient will maintain adequate hydration as evidenced by normal urine output, moist mucous membranes, and stable vital signs.

Long Term: Within the next 2-3 days, the patient will maintain balanced electrolytes and prevent further fluid loss.

Plan of Interventions

Dx:

- The nurse will monitor VS and INO every 4 hours.

- The nurse will monitor for signs of dehydration such as decreased urine output, dry mucous membranes, dry skin, and fatigue.

- The nurse will monitor electrolyte levels.

- The nurse will monitor laboratory values, including serum electrolytes (sodium, potassium, chloride), blood urea nitrogen (BUN), and creatinine.

- The nurse will monitor for any changes in the child's weight.

Tx:

- The nurse will maintain an environment of comfort and warmth, using appropriate clothing or blankets

- The nurse will encourage a balanced diet while avoiding high-sugar and high-fat foods to prevent worsening of diarrhea

- The nurse will provide medications as prescribed by the physician.

- The nurse will administer IV fluids as prescribed by the physician.

Ed:

- The nurse will encouraged to increase fluid intake.

- The nurse will advise parents to report early signs of dehydration

- The nurse will instruct parents on proper handwashing techniques before and after diaper changes and feeding times.

- The nurse will instruct parents to limit the use of fruit juices or sugary beverages during the child's recovery phase.

Rationale

Dx:

Monitor V/S and I & O

- To assess and make sure the pt's VS stays within normal ranges and to track fluid balance and prevent and detect early signs of dehydration.

Monitor for signs of dehydration

- To help detect fluid loss early, preventing complications like kidney issues and low blood pressure.

Monitor Electrolyte Levels.

- To help detect imbalances that can affect heart function, nerve signaling, and muscle contractions.

Monitor for signs of difficulty in breathing:

- To help identify respiratory distress early, allowing timely intervention to prevent worsening of conditions.

Monitor laboratory values

- These labs help assess electrolyte levels and kidney function, both of which may be affected by fluid loss from diarrhea.

Monitor for any changes in the child's weight

- Monitoring weight regularly helps track hydration status.

Tx:

Maintain an environment of comfort and warmth

- Fever or dehydration can cause the child to feel cold, so warmth ensures comfort and helps with temperature regulation.

Encourage a balanced diet while avoiding high-sugar and high-fat foods to prevent worsening of diarrhea

- Offering a balanced diet helps provide the necessary nutrients for recovery. High-sugar foods can worsen diarrhea, and fatty foods can be harder to digest.

Provide medications as prescribed by the physician.

- Medications may help manage the symptoms of diarrhea or restore normal gut flora.

Administer IV fluids

- To restore hydration, maintain electrolyte balance, and prevent complications from dehydration.

Ed:

Encourage increased fluid intake.

- To avoid signs of dehydration.

Advise parents to report early signs of dehydration

- To aid in early intervention

Instruct parents on proper handwashing techniques before and after diaper changes and feeding times.

- Good hygiene practices can help prevent the spread of infections that could be causing the diarrhea and also reduce the risk of reinfection.

Instruct parents to limit the use of fruit juices or sugary beverages during the child's recovery phase.

- Sugary drinks can worsen diarrhea by drawing water into the intestines. This increases the risk of dehydration and should be avoided while the child recovers.
