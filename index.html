Assessment Cues

S: The mother verbalized, “Naubo padin siya tas may kasamang plema.”

O: T: 36.8°C, PR: 121 bpm, RR: 28 cpm, SpO2: 98%

(+) productive cough, (+) crackles on both sides

Nursing Diagnosis

Ineffective Airway Clearance r/t retained secretions, as evidenced by adventitious lung sounds (crackles).

Objectives of Care

Short Term: By the end of the 8-hour shift, the patient will have improved airway clearance.

Long Term: Within 2-3 weeks, the patient will maintain a clear airway.

Plan of Interventions

- Monitor VS and I&O every 4 hours.

- Administer prescribed medications (Disudrin, Salbutamol, Ceftriaxone).

- Perform CPT as needed.

- Educate parents on signs of respiratory distress.

Rationale

- To ensure normal VS and track fluid balance.

- Medications help reduce congestion and fight infection.

- CPT helps clear mucus.

- Early education prevents complications.
